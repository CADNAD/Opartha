The Monastery was filled with adventure at every turn. No matter where Damin turned there was something to be learned, something to be seen. The beast that the monastery lived in sync with was, itself, a sight to behold. A huge beast, with wings as large as lakes and scaly skin the colour of dessert rock. Along the back five spines of ivory bone rose up like  sharp mountain peaks.
As Master Avador had explained, the Heralds of the Monastery lived in the spines, separated by purpose. In spine Damin and his brother resided in Heralds strove to master the physical. Using Heraldry in conjunction with martial arts to reach the pinnacle of martial prowess. In the spire where Avador lived, Heralds strove for the apotheosis of knowledge. Damin had been confined to his spire thus far, with the only exception being two trips down onto the back of the great beast.
There, Avador had showed Damin the sparse farms and villages run by the non-Heraldic members of the monastery. The base of the infrastructure that allowed them to function on the great beast.
Damin had marvelled at how the farms managed to grow food in the frigid, air sparse weather. Avador had elected not to explain it, instead forcing Damin to spend the better part of a week figuring the answer out for himself. Damin had read Terandin's five books on each Heraldic tree front to back three times before had come to a conclusion.
